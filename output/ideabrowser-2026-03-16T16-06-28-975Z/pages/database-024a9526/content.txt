Crypto inheritance platform for non-technical wallet holders
Idea of the Day Mar 16, 2026

Over $140B in crypto is locked forever. Keys lost. Passwords forgotten. Holders who died without telling anyone how to access their wallets. The tools that exist are built for developers and high-net-worth accounts with dedicated security teams. Cold wallets require tech fluency. A regular person with $30K in Bitcoin and a family that doesn't know what a seed phrase is has no good option. CryptoLegacy lets holders set up a recovery plan and name beneficiaries in minutes. The platform generates a step-by-step guide that walks a non-technical family member through accessing the assets. No seed phrases to decode. No hardware wallet interfaces to navigate. When the holder goes inactive for a set period, a dead man's switch triggers and delivers encrypted instructions to designated contacts. A spouse who has never touched crypto can follow the guide without calling a lawyer or hiring a consultant. Start with one wallet integration and a recovery guide builder. Test with 100 holders from crypto estate planning forums and Reddit threads where people ask what happens to their coins when they die. The metric: does a non-technical contact successfully complete the recovery walkthrough in a test scenario? Onboarding needs to take under ten minutes or holders won't finish. The hard part is trust. A product that stores access instructions for crypto wallets has to prove its security model before anyone hands over real credentials. Plans start at $10/month for a single wallet and scale to $30 for portfolio coverage. Crypto tax accountants are the distribution channel because they sit across the table from holders who need this. Estate planning attorneys who keep getting asked about crypto and have nothing to recommend are the second. Wallet providers who want inheritance features without building them are the partnership play. One integration with a mid-size exchange embeds CryptoLegacy into onboarding for new accounts. A crypto holder who has put this off for two years will pay $10/month the moment setup takes less time than thinking about it.

AI vocal coach for professionals who present and podcast
Released Mar 16, 2026

Sounding confident on a podcast or a Zoom call is harder than it looks. The voice goes flat. Filler words stack up. Key points get mumbled through. Traditional vocal coaching costs $100 or more per hour and requires scheduling around someone else's calendar. The gap between how a professional wants to sound and how they actually sound stays wide, and it costs credibility every time they speak. Speaksmart analyzes a speaking voice and identifies exactly what needs work. Record a 30-second clip. The app flags monotone delivery, unclear articulation, and filler word patterns. A personalized training plan builds from there - daily exercises targeting the specific issues the analysis surfaces. Progress tracking lets users hear the difference between week one and week four. Every voice pattern logged across the platform builds benchmarks by profession and use case, sharpening what good delivery actually sounds like for a sales call versus a podcast versus a boardroom. Build the analysis layer first using voice recognition APIs that score delivery across three dimensions: monotone patterns, articulation clarity, and filler word frequency. A simple mobile app where users record clips, receive scores, and get assigned daily exercises is the first useful version. Start with 20 beta users from podcasting communities where voice quality is already a topic of active discussion. The metric that matters is whether users hear themselves improving between week one and week four. Audible improvement is the only proof point that drives referrals in this market. Sessions run $15 each and monthly subscriptions run $15 to $20 for daily drills and ongoing feedback. A professional who sounds noticeably more confident after four weeks tells the next person in their network without prompting. Podcasting and public speaking communities on Reddit are where the first users come from, because that's where people already know they have a voice problem and are actively looking for a fix. Orai tracks filler words. Speeko coaches confidence. Speaksmart builds the daily practice habit that turns identified weaknesses into permanent improvements.

Training platform that creates race-specific plans for self-coached triathletes
Released Mar 16, 2026

Self-coached triathletes have two options: expensive coaches or software built for serious athletes that takes weeks to figure out. TrainingPeaks is powerful enough to manage a professional's training load. It's also complex enough to make a first-time half ironman registrant close the tab and go back to YouTube. The gap between signing up for a race and having a real plan is where people quit or get hurt. Racework generates a structured training plan built around a specific race date and a specific fitness level. Input the race distance, current weekly training hours, and how many weeks until the event. The plan builds out from there: base phase, build phase, taper. Each week adjusts to what's realistic, not what's ideal. Workouts sync to Garmin so the plan lives where training already happens. Every athlete who completes a plan and logs a race result teaches Racework what preparation actually produces finishing times worth building toward. Build a web app with a race selector and a short fitness quiz. A library of proven training blocks that auto-adjust based on athlete inputs is the core engine. Start with four race distances: Sprint, Olympic, Half Ironman, and Ironman. Get 20 triathletes to test it and track one metric: do they still follow the plan in week four. If the plan survives contact with real training schedules, the product works. If athletes abandon it, the quiz inputs need refinement before anything else gets built. Starter plans run $12 a month and pro access with nutrition guides and race-day checklists runs $30. An athlete who registers for a race has a fixed deadline and a real motivation to follow through. That commitment window is the retention mechanic no feature needs to manufacture. Triathlon communities on Reddit are where first-timers ask the same questions every season, and a plan generator that answers those questions with a structured output travels fast in threads where the alternative is contradictory advice from strangers.

Pickleball partner and court finder app
Released Mar 16, 2026

Pickleball is exploding but finding a partner and an open court still runs on group chats and hope. Facebook groups are cluttered. WhatsApp threads go stale. Players waste trips driving to facilities that turn out to be full. The sport grew to 8.9 million players, but the coordination infrastructure for casual pickup games never kept pace. Sideset shows which courts have open space and matches players by skill level and availability in real time. A player looking for a game at a specific court sees who else wants to play there and when. Courts update through player check-ins or direct facility partnerships. Players rate each other after games, keeping match quality sharp and filtering out mismatches over time. Every check-in and every rating logged builds a picture of when courts fill, which skill levels cluster where, and which players show up consistently. Build a mobile app that shows live court status and a simple matching interface. Start with three to five high-traffic courts in one metro area and seed availability data through manual check-ins before facility partnerships exist. A basic profile with skill level, preferred play times, and game history is enough to run first matches. The metric that matters is whether players who match through Sideset actually show up. No-show rate in the first month tells more about product fit than any other number. Basic access runs $4.99 a month and pro membership with advanced filtering and priority matching during peak hours runs $19.99. The player who finds a reliable weekly partner through Sideset has no reason to go back to coordinating through group chats. Pickleball communities on Reddit and at local facilities are where the first users come from, because that's where players already complain about showing up to empty courts and missed connections. The app solves a problem every player in those threads has had at least once.

Genetic-risk screening platform for full-family testing
Idea of the Day Mar 15, 2026

Seven out of ten at-risk family members never get tested. Not because they refuse, but because the outreach never reaches them. A genetic counselor identifies a hereditary cancer risk, and cascade screening stalls at the point of coordination. Letters go unsent. Calls go unreturned. Family members who would have said yes never get the chance. Lineage AI takes the coordination off the counselor's desk. Upload a patient's test results and the platform maps the family tree, identifies at-risk relatives, and handles outreach through text, email, or letter. Each message is timed and personalized, reaching family members in the format most likely to get a response. Consent, scheduling, and compliance logging follow automatically. The counselor manages one dashboard instead of fifty spreadsheets. Start with five genetic counseling practices focused on hereditary cancer, where cascade screening protocols are well-defined and reimbursement pathways already exist. Build a web dashboard that ingests test results and lets counselors map family structures by hand during the first version. HIPAA-compliant messaging handles outreach across text, email, and letter. A lightweight CRM underneath tracks who's been contacted, who responded, and who completed testing. The metric is cascade completion rate against the industry baseline of 30 percent. Anything above that in the first cohort proves the coordination was the bottleneck all along. Genetic counseling practices pay $500 to $2,000 a month based on patient volume. Health systems with in-house genetics departments license it for $25,000 to $100,000 annually. Payer partnerships come next. Insurers save money when at-risk patients get tested before they develop cancer rather than after. Hereditary cancer is the entry point. Cardiovascular genetics, pharmacogenomics, and rare disease registries expand the platform into adjacent verticals where cascade screening has the same coordination problem. Cascade rates have been stuck at 30 percent for years. The coordination is the bottleneck, and everything on the other side of fixing it is growth.

Premium access runs $5 a month for ad-free viewing, personalized learning paths, and exclusive content. Flight compensation recovery agent for frequent flyers
Idea of the Day Mar 14, 2026

Airlines don't make it easy to collect what they owe. Regulations mandate compensation for qualifying delays. Airlines reject most first-attempt claims, counting on travelers not to follow up. The claims industry that sprang up around this takes 35% and only engages after the disruption is over. For frequent flyers, the bigger cost isn't the missed payout. It's the hour on hold rebooking instead of prepping for the meeting. Reroute monitors flights in real time and acts the moment something breaks. A cancellation triggers instant rebooking based on stored preferences for seat, loyalty program, and connections. The platform checks whether the delay qualifies for compensation, assembles documentation, and files the claim. A text lands before the gate agent finishes the first in line. Outcome data feeds back into the filing engine, sharpening which claims succeed by airline, route, and disruption type. The first build is a monitoring layer connected to public airline data feeds. When a disruption hits, the system matches it against compensation rules for the relevant carrier and generates a prefilled claim. Rebooking logic layers on top, pulling stored preferences to rank alternatives by loyalty program, seat type, and connection risk. The claims engine is the hardest piece. Airlines reject on technicalities and slow-walk payouts. Starting with European carriers makes sense because payout rules are standardized. U.S. regulations are still carrier-specific, so filing logic needs to be modular from day one. Individuals pay $29 per month. Corporate teams scale to $10 to $30 per user, plus 25% commission on successful claims. That commission model means Reroute only gets paid when the traveler does. Travel managers get disruption cost visibility through expense tool integrations. Rebooking patterns reveal which routes break most often, building toward predictive risk scoring that corporate travel desks will pay a premium for. The largest claims service only shows up after the damage is done. Reroute starts before the bags stop moving.

Companies maintaining legacy machinery have no good options between replacing the entire system and hoping a $12K component from eBay actually works. Parts Vault is a marketplace where every rare industrial component gets verified before money moves. Sellers list parts with detailed specs and photos.
